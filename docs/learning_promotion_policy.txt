1. FORMAL ROLE OF THIS LAYER
This layer defines promotion readiness and non-promotion discipline for governed learning outputs.
This layer does not activate runtime change.
This layer does not write into the active plan.
This layer does not convert promotion readiness into implementation.
This layer does not alter strategy logic, thresholds, governors, failure detectors, execution-quality behavior, dirty-environment handling, trend-continuation logic, or any live trading behavior.

2. WHAT PROMOTION MEANS IN THIS PHASE

4. FORMAL PROMOTION CRITERIA
A governed learning output may be considered promotion-ready only when all of the following are true:
- sufficient and traceable evidence basis exists for the specific bounded claim.
- review-governed acceptance history exists and remains consistent with L4 decision discipline.
- experiment design or review support exists where applicable under L5.
- scope is bounded, explicit, and does not conceal broad or cross-layer mutation intent.
- risk framing is explicit, credible, and proportionate to the claim.
- validation expectations are explicit and reviewable.
- rollback readiness is explicit, feasible, and traceable.
- no prohibited mutation path is present.
- no unresolved critical ambiguity remains.
- no authority elevation implication exists.
- no hidden runtime activation intent exists.

5. PROMOTION SIGN-OFF EXPECTATIONS
Lower-risk and tightly bounded items may be considered promotion-candidate only after REVIEW_BOARD sign-off.
Governance-sensitive, cross-scope, or materially consequential items may require stronger escalation and may require COMMITTEE_REVIEWER visibility or sign-off when justified.
Sign-off in this phase means governance readiness only.
Sign-off in this phase does not authorize runtime activation, direct apply, or plan overwrite.

required_signoff_default=REVIEW_BOARD
required_signoff_elevated=COMMITTEE_REVIEWER_WHEN_GOVERNANCE_OR_MATERIALITY_JUSTIFIES
signoff_rule=SIGNOFF_CONFIRMS_GOVERNED_PROMOTION_READINESS_ONLY

6. NON-PROMOTION PRINCIPLE
A learning output that fails any critical promotion criterion remains non-promotion-ready.
A persuasive narrative, sophisticated presentation, or lengthy analysis does not substitute for evidence sufficiency, validation framing, rollback readiness, or governance sign-off.
Promotion pressure from weak evidence is forbidden.
