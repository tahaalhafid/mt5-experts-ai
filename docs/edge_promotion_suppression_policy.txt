phase_id=E9

purpose:
- define how edge families may later be promoted, tightened, narrowed, downgraded, or suppressed using factory evidence
- preserve a governance-only posture in this phase

core_definition:
- promotion means evidence-based governance advancement only; it does not mean runtime activation.
- suppression means governance restriction pressure only; it does not mean live disabling in E9.
- tightening means later demanding stronger evidence, cleaner timing, better geometry, or cleaner contextual conditions before broader acceptance.
- narrowing means later reducing the family's acceptable scope to fewer zones, regimes, or contexts.
- downgrade means later reducing family standing or confidence posture when evidence does not justify current breadth.
- all E9 governance remains non-runtime, non-executing, non-licensing, and non-mutative.

hard_boundaries:
- E9 does not change current runtime licensing
- E9 does not rewrite current council behavior
- E9 does not suppress families live
- E9 does not promote families live
- E9 does not bypass later review or enrichment phases

conceptual_governance_inputs:
- zone canon
- regime posture
- timing structure
- invalidation and risk geometry
- exit architecture
- scorecards and runtime accounting
- health posture
- deterioration posture
- suppression pressure
- overblocking risk
- geometry quality
- evidence depth

promotion_readiness_guidance:
- promotion readiness is later justified only when thesis coherence, regime fit, timing clarity, invalidation clarity, geometry quality, exit coherence, and evidence depth are strong enough together.
- absence of strong suppression pressure matters as much as positive structure.
- scorecards contribute evidence; they do not self-govern.

suppression_and_downgrade_guidance:
- suppression pressure should surface when fragility, persistent poor geometry, deterioration, evidence weakness, vague edge logic, or inflated scope persist.
- downgrade pressure should surface before silent demotion; the pressure must remain visible and reviewable.
- tightening and narrowing are preferred when the family still has strategic value but current scope is too broad or too weakly evidenced.

anti-overreach_rules:
- no family is promoted merely because it is a current canon seed
- no family is suppressed merely because it is conditional
- no single strong structural description rescues repeated poor geometry or weak evidence depth
- governance pressure remains descriptive until later review/governance phases act on it
