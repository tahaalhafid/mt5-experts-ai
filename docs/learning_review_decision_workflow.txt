GLOBAL GOVERNANCE PRINCIPLES
- Learning proposals remain bounded, reviewable, non-authoritative artifacts.
- OPERATOR may view and flag learning proposals but may not approve them.
- ADMIN_REVIEWER may triage, reject, defer, request more evidence, and escalate within governed review.
- REVIEW_BOARD handles structured learning proposals that require formal governed consideration.
- COMMITTEE_REVIEWER is reserved for justified governance-sensitive escalations only.
- No learning review decision in this phase creates runtime mutation authority.
- No learning proposal acceptance equals live activation, plan overwrite, threshold mutation, or auto-apply.
- Review-governed acceptance produces handoff eligibility only for later planning or later controlled experimentation consideration.
- Proposal review must remain traceable, bounded, and closed explicitly to avoid silent limbo.

REVIEWER ASSIGNMENT PRINCIPLES
- proposal_family_name, proposal_risk_class, and required_review_level determine the primary reviewer role.
- LOW_RISK_REVIEW proposals usually begin with ADMIN_REVIEWER.
- HIGH_RISK_REVIEW and GOVERNANCE_SENSITIVE_REVIEW proposals normally require REVIEW_BOARD handling even if triaged first by ADMIN_REVIEWER.
- COMMITTEE_REVIEWER is used only when governance-boundary consequences, institutional contradictions, or policy-scope consequences justify higher review.
- Weak evidence alone does not justify escalation; weak proposals may be rejected or returned for more evidence instead.

PRE-REVIEW TRIAGE RULES
- Reject before deeper review if hidden mutation intent is present.
- Reject before deeper review if evidence is thin for the claim scope.
- Require more evidence if the idea is plausible but support is currently insufficient.
- Defer if the proposal is bounded and potentially useful but timing, priority, or contextual readiness is not adequate.
- Escalate if review scope exceeds ADMIN_REVIEWER comfort, if governance sensitivity is material, or if risk framing requires REVIEW_BOARD handling.
- Close with no action if the proposal adds no governed next-step value under the current window or scope.

DECISION DISCIPLINE
- REJECT means no governed next step proceeds from the current proposal package.
- DEFER means the proposal is not rejected, but it cannot progress now without changed timing, context, or priority.
- REQUIRE_MORE_EVIDENCE means the proposal may re-enter review only after stronger or better-linked evidence is added.
- ACCEPT_FOR_EXPERIMENT means later controlled experimentation consideration may proceed in a future phase; it does not launch anything here.
- ACCEPT_FOR_GOVERNED_ACTION_PREPARATION means later planning or structured preparation may proceed in a future phase; it does not authorize mutation or activation here.
- ESCALATE means ownership moves to a higher or more appropriate reviewer role with explicit reason.
- CLOSE_NO_ACTION means the proposal is archived without current next-step continuation.

ESCALATION RULES
- insufficient_admin_confidence=ESCALATE_TO_REVIEW_BOARD

DEFER AND REJECT DISCIPLINE
- defer_when=boundedness_is_sound_but_timing_priority_or_context_is_not_yet_right
- reject_when=hidden_mutation_path_exists,evidence_is_too_weak_for_claim,traceability_is_missing,boundedness_is_missing,risk_framing_is_unsound
- require_more_evidence_when=idea_is_plausible_but_bundle_strength_linkage_or_corroboration_is_not_yet_sufficient
- repeated_defer_limit=do_not_allow_endless_defer_chains_without_new_evidence_or_new_context
- repeated_weak_proposal_rule=close_under_current_window_or_scope_if_new_support_does_not_arrive

RELATIONSHIP TO L5 AND LATER PHASES
- L4 outputs feed later L5 controlled experimentation governance.
- L4 may produce experiment-ready or governed-action-preparation-ready handoff states only.
- L4 does not launch experiments.
- L4 does not promote learning into runtime.
- L4 does not bypass review boundaries, repository discipline, or human decision accountability.

AI ROLE BOUNDARY
- AI may support proposal clarity, summarize evidence for reviewers, and organize bounded rationale.
- AI may not decide review outcomes.
- AI may not self-escalate into approval.
- AI may not convert review decisions into runtime changes.
- AI may not bypass human reviewer roles or create silent acceptance.
