============================================================
WHAT ZONE-BASED CANON MEANS
============================================================
Zone-based canon means:
- edge material is organized into canonical market-behavior zones rather than kept in one undifferentiated pool
- each zone acts as an organizational container for related edge families, support primitives, market-native context, and reference material
- zones describe behavioral neighborhoods, not executable runtime permissions

Zone-based canon does NOT mean:
- final runtime licensing
- regime permission
- execution approval
- trigger approval
- automatic canon permanence
- profitability proof

============================================================
CANONICAL ZONES
============================================================
The formal canonical zones in E3 are:
- TREND_CONTINUATION
- TREND_PULLBACK
- RANGE_MEAN_RECLAIM
- REVERSAL_EXHAUSTION
- COMPRESSION
- EXPANSION_BREAKOUT
- EXPANSION_CONTINUATION
- MICRO_RANGE_EXPANSION

Contextual semantic states such as RANGE_BALANCED, RANGE_DIRTY, UNKNOWN, or NO_TRADE are not promoted here as canonical factory zones.
They remain contextual or reference-only descriptors rather than primary canon containers.

============================================================
CONSTRUCTION RULES
============================================================
1) A material may have one primary zone and zero or more secondary zones.
2) Primary zone membership should stay conservative and sparse.
3) Not every material deserves primary family membership.
4) Primitive support must remain primitive support.
5) Market-native objects may support zones as raw context objects only.
6) Legacy and research-only references may remain attached to zones without becoming canon drivers.
7) Zone membership organizes behavior families around market logic; it does not determine runtime permission.

============================================================
ANTI-BLOAT / ANTI-DUPLICATION
============================================================
- One zone may legitimately have only one strong family seed.
- Overlapping family names should not multiply canon clutter.
- Old library identities should not compete directly with current family seeds unless re-validated later.
- Primitives must not masquerade as families.
- Crowded labels are not a sign of canon strength.
