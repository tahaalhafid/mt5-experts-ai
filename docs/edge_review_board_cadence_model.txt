EDGE REVIEW BOARD CADENCE MODEL — E11

Cadence principles
- Cadence is strategic and conservative.
- Cadence is designed to review the factory’s trajectory, not to steer runtime behavior.
- Reviews may be periodic or escalation-driven.

Cadence classes
1) PERIODIC_STRATEGIC_REVIEW
- broad strategic scan of the active edge factory
- reviews current canon balance, concentration, strategic focus, and major weak points
- suitable cadence: periodic program review

2) DETERIORATION_ESCALATION_REVIEW
- used when fragility, weak evidence depth, poor geometry interaction, or unresolved governance pressure clusters around specific families
- suitable cadence: triggered by repeated weak signals or meaningful concern clusters

3) COVERAGE_GAP_REVIEW
- used when zones remain persistently sparse or underseeded
- suitable cadence: periodic and after meaningful intake/enrichment changes

4) OVERLAP_CROWDING_REVIEW
- used when overlapping labels or family duplication threatens canon clarity
- suitable cadence: periodic or on overlap escalation

5) ENRICHMENT_VALUE_REVIEW
- used when enrichment loops appear productive or noisy
- suitable cadence: after enrichment batches or repeated low-value reentry signals

6) SUPPRESSION_ESCALATION_REVIEW
- used when suppression pressure becomes materially visible and may justify later action preparation
- suitable cadence: event-driven, not continuous runtime control

Cadence boundary
- Cadence produces strategic review outcomes only.
- Cadence does not grant live approval, live suppression, live promotion, or code mutation.
