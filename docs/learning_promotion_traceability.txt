3. TRACEABILITY QUALITY EXPECTATIONS
Traceability must preserve a clear chain from evidence to proposal to review outcome to experiment-design support where applicable to promotion-readiness assessment.
Traceability must make it possible to explain why a learning output was or was not considered promotion-ready.
Traceability must surface remaining limitations and unresolved ambiguity honestly.
Traceability must remain bounded and must not be repurposed as a hidden mutation or implementation channel.

4. RELATIONSHIP TO LATER IMPLEMENTATION
This phase may only prepare governed promotion readiness.
This phase does not activate runtime change.
This phase does not write into the active plan.
This phase does not bypass future implementation governance.
This phase feeds later L7 impact audit and future implementation planning outside this phase.

5. AI ROLE BOUNDARY AT PROMOTION STAGE
AI may summarize promotion rationale, remaining limitations, and rollback readiness references.
AI may not self-promote outputs.
AI may not self-signoff.
AI may not convert promotion readiness into implementation.
AI may not weaken rollback discipline.
AI may not create implicit authority elevation.
