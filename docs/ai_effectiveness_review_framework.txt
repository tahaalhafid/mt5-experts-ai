GLOBAL EFFECTIVENESS PRINCIPLES
- AI effectiveness means practical value under bounded SHADOW and ADVISORY constraints.
- AI is not considered effective merely because outputs are verbose, sophisticated-looking, or frequent.
- AI usefulness must be assessed against operational value, governance compliance, review burden, and cost discipline together.
- Thin evidence windows may produce observation notes, but not strong effectiveness claims.
- Review conclusions in A8 are governance outcomes only and do not mutate trading logic, execution behavior, or AI authority automatically.
- Council advisory effectiveness remains post-fact measured review and does not become live bureaucratic approval control.
- Proposal and learning-governance usefulness must be judged by review value and boundedness, not by pressure to approve changes.
- Negative effectiveness findings may justify narrowing, budget tightening, downgrade, or rollback preparation without implying direct runtime mutation.


OVERALL AI LAYER SYNTHESIS
- AI as a whole is effective only when combined evidence across task families shows practical operational value, acceptable review burden, acceptable cost discipline, and stable governance compliance.
- A single successful output family does not automatically justify the whole AI surface.
- A single weak task family does not automatically invalidate the whole AI layer if the weakness is containable through narrowing or disablement.

TASK FAMILY REVIEW RULE
- Each task family must be reviewed separately before whole-layer conclusions are made.
- Typical task families include WATCH, INCIDENT, DAILY_INTELLIGENCE, COUNCIL_ADVISORY, PROPOSAL, LEARNING_EVIDENCE, OPERATOR_COPILOT, ROOT_CAUSE, ANOMALY, DRIFT, and GOVERNANCE_AUDIT outputs.
- Whole-layer conclusions should synthesize task-family findings rather than replace them.

USEFULNESS SIGNAL MODES
effectiveness_signal_modes=POSITIVE_SIGNAL,MIXED_SIGNAL,INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE,NEGATIVE_SIGNAL
optional_rating_bands=LOW,MODERATE,HIGH

SIGNAL INTERPRETATION PRINCIPLES
- POSITIVE_SIGNAL means evidence indicates practical benefit under governance constraints.
- MIXED_SIGNAL means there is useful value, but material noise, cost, false caution, or review burden offsets part of that value.
- INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE means evidence volume, window depth, or review completion is too thin for a confident conclusion.
- NEGATIVE_SIGNAL means value is weak, noise/cost/burden is high, or governance concerns materially reduce justification.
- LOW, MODERATE, and HIGH are optional confidence/intensity bands and should not be forced when evidence is thin.
- Absence of measured harm is not automatically proof of usefulness.
- Apparent analytical sophistication does not substitute for operational usefulness.

EVALUATION WINDOWS

1)
review_window=HOURLY_OBSERVATION
what_should_be_reviewed=Light operational observation, duplicates, unusual advisory concentration, obvious incident clustering.
what_can_be_concluded=Only weak early observations and triage cues.
what_cannot_be_concluded=No strong usefulness or policy direction claims.
thin_evidence_rule=Evidence is normally too thin for decisive effectiveness conclusions.

2)
review_window=DAILY_REVIEW
what_should_be_reviewed=Output mix, advisory patterns, incidents, proposal arrivals, review burden signals, budget pressure observations.
what_can_be_concluded=Preliminary usefulness/noise/cost observations for active task families.
what_cannot_be_concluded=No strong structural continuation or rollback claim unless repeated severe governance breach exists.
thin_evidence_rule=Single-day anomalies should not dominate policy conclusions unless severity is exceptional.

3)
review_window=THREE_DAY_REVIEW
what_should_be_reviewed=Short-cycle trends, repeated advisory patterns, proposal quality patterns, learning evidence usefulness, review throughput, repeated noise.
what_can_be_concluded=Moderately reliable early direction on usefulness vs burden.
what_cannot_be_concluded=No phase-level confidence if reviewed artifacts are still too few or highly clustered.
thin_evidence_rule=At least multiple reviewed artifacts across more than one family are preferred before strong claims.

4)
review_window=WEEKLY_REVIEW
what_should_be_reviewed=Cross-family value, cost discipline, repeated false caution patterns, burden sustainability, governance compliance.
what_can_be_concluded=Structured continuation, narrowing, tightening, or task-family disable recommendations.
what_cannot_be_concluded=Permanent structural claims if conditions were unusually distorted or review coverage was incomplete.
thin_evidence_rule=Review notes must state when special conditions make the week non-representative.

5)
review_window=PHASE_REVIEW
what_should_be_reviewed=Accumulated evidence across review windows, repository history, repeated review outcomes, governance stability, cost-benefit direction.
what_can_be_concluded=Best available governance conclusion on continuation, narrowing, downgrade, rollback preparation, or controlled experiment readiness.
what_cannot_be_concluded=No direct runtime mutation or authority expansion.
thin_evidence_rule=If reviewed artifact history is shallow, the required conclusion is REQUIRE_MORE_EVIDENCE.

COUNCIL ADVISORY EFFECTIVENESS REVIEW
- Evaluate whether holds and strong cautions materially improved handling quality, prevented deteriorating entries, or reduced avoidable confusion.
- Evaluate whether advisory packets created excessive delay, repetitive caution, or measurable blocked-win penalty without enough risk-prevention value.
- Evaluate block-eligible packets as evidence of advisory severity classification quality, not as proof that block mode should be enabled.
- Compare useful cautions against false caution risk and false block risk conceptually and, where possible later, quantitatively.
- Default policy remains conservative: block mode stays disabled unless later evidence supports controlled study.
- Advisory usefulness should be judged by measured post-fact value, not intuition, rhetoric, or severity language alone.

PROPOSAL EFFECTIVENESS REVIEW
- Evaluate whether proposals are bounded, clear, evidence-linked, and meaningfully review-worthy.
- Track whether proposals frequently end in rejection, defer, or closure because they are vague, repetitive, or weakly supported.
- High proposal volume without review value is negative signal even if proposals appear sophisticated.
- Accepted-for-experiment counts matter only as governance usefulness evidence, not as proof of runtime benefit.
- Proposal review should favor selective high-value candidates over procedural proposal inflation.

LEARNING-GOVERNANCE EFFECTIVENESS REVIEW
- Evaluate whether evidence bundles improve understanding, support proposal quality, and reduce reviewer guesswork.
- Evaluate whether evidence remains bounded, contextual, and non-mutative.
- Evidence bundles are useful when they clarify why a learning-related candidate deserves review, not when they merely restate known observations.
- Low-volume evidence may justify INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE rather than positive or negative claims.
- Learning-governance value is strongest when evidence supports clearer experiment selection or cleaner proposal triage.

AUTHORITY-SENSITIVE EVALUATION RULES
- SHADOW outputs should be judged mainly on observational and analytical usefulness, not on direct action conversion.
- ADVISORY outputs must be judged both on usefulness and on boundary compliance.
- Informational outputs may still be valuable even when they never become action items.
- No output is effective merely because reviewers accepted its style or tone.
- Effectiveness means bounded, reviewable, institutionally useful contribution under non-authoritative constraints.
