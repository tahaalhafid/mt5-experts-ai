exit_states_defined:
- EXIT_NOT_APPLICABLE
- EXIT_TARGET_1_ELIGIBLE
- EXIT_TARGET_2_ELIGIBLE
- EXIT_PARTIAL_REDUCTION_ELIGIBLE
- EXIT_TIME_STOP_ELIGIBLE
- EXIT_FAILURE_TO_EXPAND_ELIGIBLE
- EXIT_PROTECTIVE_TIGHTENING_ELIGIBLE
- EXIT_STRUCTURAL_TRAIL_ELIGIBLE
- EXIT_MOMENTUM_DECAY_ELIGIBLE
- EXIT_EXHAUSTION_PROTECTIVE_ELIGIBLE

state_meanings:

EXIT_NOT_APPLICABLE:
- use when regime posture is suppressed or the family should not be modeled as structurally active in that context

EXIT_TARGET_1_ELIGIBLE:
- first meaningful harvesting objective is conceptually available

EXIT_TARGET_2_ELIGIBLE:
- secondary/stretched objective may be justified if the path remains structurally healthy

EXIT_PARTIAL_REDUCTION_ELIGIBLE:
- reduce exposure without declaring thesis failure

EXIT_TIME_STOP_ELIGIBLE:
- the edge may still be alive, but time efficiency has degraded enough to justify exit consideration

EXIT_FAILURE_TO_EXPAND_ELIGIBLE:
- trigger/entry occurred, but the move failed to travel as the thesis path required

EXIT_PROTECTIVE_TIGHTENING_ELIGIBLE:
- tighten protection while thesis remains partially intact

EXIT_STRUCTURAL_TRAIL_ELIGIBLE:
- use structure-aware trailing/holding logic rather than fixed profit harvesting only

EXIT_MOMENTUM_DECAY_ELIGIBLE:
- continuation or expansion quality has decayed enough to justify more defensive management

EXIT_EXHAUSTION_PROTECTIVE_ELIGIBLE:
- exhaustion or snapback risk justifies faster protective behavior even before full invalidation

state_discipline:
- exit state eligibility does not equal runtime order behavior
- protective states are not the same as invalidation
- time-stop eligibility is not proof of thesis failure
- failure-to-expand eligibility is especially important for breakout/release and micro-expansion structures
- not every family should rely on target-1/target-2 only
