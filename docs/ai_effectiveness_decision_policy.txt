EFFECTIVENESS CONCLUSION OUTCOMES
- CONTINUE_AS_IS
  meaning=Current task family or AI layer remains justified under present bounded policy.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

- CONTINUE_WITH_TIGHTER_BUDGETS
  meaning=Value exists, but budget or depth consumption should be reduced in later policy handling.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

- CONTINUE_WITH_NARROWER_SCOPE
  meaning=Value exists, but triggers, cadence, or output families should be narrowed in future governed tuning.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

- ADVISORY_ONLY_KEEP
  meaning=Keep bounded advisory usefulness where shadow/proposal/evidence value is weaker.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

- SHADOW_ONLY_DOWNGRADE
  meaning=Usefulness is insufficient for current advisory layer, but observational/shadow value may still justify retention.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

- HOLD_FEATURE_EXPANSION
  meaning=Do not widen AI responsibilities until stronger effectiveness evidence exists.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

- REQUIRE_MORE_EVIDENCE
  meaning=Evidence is too thin or mixed for a stable governance conclusion.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

- PREPARE_CONTROLLED_EXPERIMENT
  meaning=Evidence may justify designing a later tightly bounded experiment, subject to separate governance and not auto-enabled here.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

- DISABLE_SPECIFIC_TASK_FAMILY
  meaning=A specific task family is not sufficiently justified and should be considered for disablement through later governed change process.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

- DISABLE_SPECIFIC_ADVISORY_MODE
  meaning=A specific advisory severity pattern or advisory handling mode should be considered for disablement through later governed change process.
  direct_runtime_mutation=false

OUTCOME SELECTION PRINCIPLES
- CONTINUE_AS_IS requires positive usefulness with acceptable burden, cost, and boundary compliance.
- CONTINUE_WITH_TIGHTER_BUDGETS applies when value exists but budget pressure or investigation depth is too costly.
- CONTINUE_WITH_NARROWER_SCOPE applies when a task family is helpful only under a more selective trigger set.
- ADVISORY_ONLY_KEEP applies when advisory retains value but broader AI surface does not yet justify more ambitious continuation.
- SHADOW_ONLY_DOWNGRADE applies when advisory usefulness is weak or noisy but passive observation still has review value.
- HOLD_FEATURE_EXPANSION is the default when evidence is mixed and governance confidence is not strong.
- REQUIRE_MORE_EVIDENCE is mandatory when windows are thin, review coverage is incomplete, or counterfactual claims are too speculative.
- PREPARE_CONTROLLED_EXPERIMENT may be recommended only when evidence is positive enough to justify later tightly bounded study.
- DISABLE_* outcomes are appropriate when repeated low value, governance concern, or burden-cost imbalance materially outweighs benefit.

EVALUATION RULES BY SIGNAL
- POSITIVE_SIGNAL -> eligible outcomes: CONTINUE_AS_IS, CONTINUE_WITH_TIGHTER_BUDGETS, CONTINUE_WITH_NARROWER_SCOPE, PREPARE_CONTROLLED_EXPERIMENT
- MIXED_SIGNAL -> eligible outcomes: CONTINUE_WITH_TIGHTER_BUDGETS, CONTINUE_WITH_NARROWER_SCOPE, HOLD_FEATURE_EXPANSION, REQUIRE_MORE_EVIDENCE, SHADOW_ONLY_DOWNGRADE
- INSUFFICIENT_EVIDENCE -> eligible outcomes: REQUIRE_MORE_EVIDENCE, HOLD_FEATURE_EXPANSION
- NEGATIVE_SIGNAL -> eligible outcomes: SHADOW_ONLY_DOWNGRADE, DISABLE_SPECIFIC_TASK_FAMILY, DISABLE_SPECIFIC_ADVISORY_MODE, HOLD_FEATURE_EXPANSION

COUNCIL ADVISORY EFFECTIVENESS POLICY
- Council advisory is effective when it improves decision handling quality without creating excessive delay, caution inflation, or procedural drag.
- Useful holds should be evidenced by better post-fact handling quality, plausible deterioration avoidance, or more disciplined reevaluation.
- Strong caution is not automatically effective simply because it sounds prudent; it must show measured benefit or at least credible review value.
- Block-eligible classification should be reviewed as severity evidence only while block mode remains disabled by default.
- Repeated false caution, repeated blocked-win penalty, or repetitive hold-without-value patterns justify MIXED_SIGNAL or NEGATIVE_SIGNAL findings.
- Even strong positive advisory evidence does not auto-enable block mode. At most it may support PREPARE_CONTROLLED_EXPERIMENT for later separate governance study.

PROPOSAL EFFECTIVENESS POLICY
- Proposal quality is positive when proposals are bounded, clear, evidence-linked, review-worthy, and selectively useful.
- High rejection or defer rates do not automatically imply failure, but repeated vagueness, repetition, or weak evidence does.
- Proposal fatigue is a negative signal even when some proposals appear intelligent.
- Proposal usefulness should be judged by review value, prioritization clarity, and experiment/action planning readiness under governance.
- A proposal accepted for experiment is a governance usefulness signal, not proof of live profitability or runtime correctness.

LEARNING-GOVERNANCE EFFECTIVENESS POLICY
- Learning evidence is effective when it improves reviewer understanding, strengthens proposal quality, and reduces guesswork.
- Evidence bundles that remain archival but materially clarify review decisions can still be positive.
- Evidence bundles that merely restate obvious observations or create review clutter are mixed or negative signals.
- Learning-governance usefulness must remain bounded and non-mutative; a mutative tendency is a governance problem, not a sign of effectiveness.
- Too few reviewed evidence bundles require REQUIRE_MORE_EVIDENCE rather than optimistic assumptions.

FALSE-POSITIVE AND FALSE-COST DISCIPLINE
- false_caution discipline: repeated caution without corresponding risk-prevention value counts against advisory usefulness.
- false_block discipline: repeated block-eligible severity without meaningful post-fact justification counts against future block-mode credibility.
- excessive_hold discipline: repeated holds that mainly create delay without improved handling quality count against advisory value.
- repeated_low_value_alert discipline: drift/anomaly/root-cause outputs that repeat without new review value count as noise.
- proposal_spam discipline: proposal volume without bounded review value counts as negative signal.
- review_fatigue discipline: when reviewers spend time closing low-value items repeatedly, review burden must weigh heavily in conclusions.
- cost_without_insight discipline: expensive or deep investigations that add little decision value count against continuation.
- intelligent_text_is_not_enough rule: polished narrative quality does not offset weak value, excessive noise, or governance friction.

AUTHORITY AND GOVERNANCE DISCIPLINE
- SHADOW outputs should not be penalized merely for not becoming action items.
- ADVISORY outputs must be assessed for both usefulness and boundary compliance.
- Governance compliance is a hard constraint. An output family with repeated authority boundary breaches cannot be judged strongly effective.
- No effectiveness conclusion authorizes direct control, auto-apply, direct BUY/SELL generation, or silent plan mutation.
- No effectiveness conclusion in A8 changes trading logic, activation status, or block-mode state automatically.

EVIDENCE SUFFICIENCY RULES
- Thin daily evidence should usually lead to observation notes or REQUIRE_MORE_EVIDENCE.
- Strong phase conclusions require reviewed artifacts across relevant families, not isolated anecdotes.
- Counterfactual claims such as prevented loss or blocked win should be framed conservatively.
- When review completion is sparse, usefulness certainty must remain low.
- Severe governance breaches are one of the few cases that may justify strong negative action even with thinner evidence.
