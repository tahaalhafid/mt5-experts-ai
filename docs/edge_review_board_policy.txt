EDGE REVIEW BOARD POLICY — E11

Purpose
- The Institutional Edge Review Board is the strategic oversight layer of the edge factory.
- It reviews edge quality, concentration, fragility, overlap, suppression pressure, enrichment value, and strategic fit.
- It does not act as runtime council logic, AI authority escalation, live trading control, or auto-promotion/auto-suppression.

Boundary
- E11 is governance-only.
- It is non-runtime, non-executing, non-licensing, and non-mutative.
- Review-board outputs do not change trading behavior, runtime permissions, or code paths by themselves.

What the board reviews
- current canon status
- zone coverage
- regime mapping
- timing and trigger architecture
- invalidation and risk geometry
- exit architecture
- scorecards and runtime accounting
- governance postures
- enrichment signals
- market-native concentration or gap signals
- inventory crowding, duplication, and strategic gap signals

Review-board roles
- BOARD_CHAIR
  - owns agenda discipline and boundary protection
  - may frame strategic review scope
  - may not create runtime permissions
- STRATEGIC_EDGE_REVIEWER
  - reviews strategic edge quality, concentration, fragility, and fit
  - may recommend strategic outcomes only
- EVIDENCE_CURATOR
  - assembles inputs from canon, regime, timing, risk, exit, scorecards, governance, and enrichment layers
  - may not act as promotion/suppression authority
- GOVERNANCE_SECRETARIAT
  - records review findings, outcomes, and lineage
  - may not create live control

Strategic concerns the board is expected to surface
- concentrated dependence on too few strong families
- persistent sparse zones
- repeated family fragility without resolution
- overlap/crowding that inflates canon clutter
- suppression pressure that may justify later action-preparation review
- enrichment loops producing low strategic value
- future productization focus versus deprioritization candidates

Required truth discipline
- good narrative quality is not equal to strong strategic value
- repeated governance motion is not equal to strategic progress
- overlap should remain explicit and bounded
- weak families should not gain status merely because they are adjacent to stronger ones
