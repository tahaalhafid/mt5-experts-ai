artifact_role=EDGE_SCORECARD_POLICY
artifact_authority_class=AUTHORITATIVE_FACTORY_SCORECARD_POLICY
policy_version=E8_EDGE_SCORECARD_POLICY_V1
phase_id=E8

purpose:
- define edge scorecards as the formal measurement layer for edge families inside the factory
- define runtime accounting as the later evidence-feed model for attempts, executions, rejects, holds, and outcome quality
- preserve a non-runtime, non-governing posture in this phase

core_definition:
- an edge scorecard is the structured measurement record for a family or family-zone context.
- runtime accounting is the later process by which runtime evidence is attributed back into those scorecards over time.
- scorecards describe how an edge should be measured; they do not yet compute live scores, suppress families, or promote families.

hard_boundaries:
- E8 is non-runtime
- E8 is non-executing
- E8 is non-governing
- E8 does not rewrite current journaling behavior
- E8 does not create live suppression behavior
- E8 does not replace later promotion/suppression governance

required_scorecard_categories:
- attempts
- candidates
- executions
- rejects
- holds
- win_loss_outcomes
- expectancy
- mae
- mfe
- regime_fit_quality
- session_fit_quality
- deterioration_signal
- suppression_pressure
- overblocking_risk
- execution_quality_interaction

core_principles:
- edge families must be measured, not merely described
- structural elegance alone is not evidence of edge health
- one attractive trigger does not cancel poor geometry
- one good narrative does not cancel concentration or deterioration risk
- runtime evidence must later feed scorecards through governed attribution, not through uncontrolled self-promotion

anti_control_rule:
- scorecards do not directly suppress families in E8
- scorecards do not directly promote families in E8
- scorecards do not imply runtime permission
- scorecards prepare the evidence base for later governance only

status_model_intent:
- SCORECARD_PLACEHOLDER_ONLY is reserved for architecture that has not yet been made feed-ready
- SCORECARD_READY_FOR_RUNTIME_FEED means the scorecard structure is coherent and can accept later accounting inputs
- SCORECARD_RUNTIME_FEED_PENDING means the family/context is structurally known but still weak enough that later feed quality must be treated carefully
- SCORECARD_ACTIVE_ACCOUNTING_READY means the family/context is structurally mature enough to anchor later runtime accounting without implying current live scoring
- SCORECARD_REVIEW_SIGNAL_PRESENT means the family/context already carries ambiguity, crowding, or fragility signals that later governance should watch closely

boundary_note:
- scorecards remain measurement architecture only until later phases integrate governance, exports, and approved runtime use.
