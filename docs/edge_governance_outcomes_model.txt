governance_posture_classes:
- PROMOTION_READY
- PROMOTION_CANDIDATE
- MAINTAIN_AS_IS
- TIGHTENING_RECOMMENDED
- NARROWING_RECOMMENDED
- DOWNGRADE_RECOMMENDED
- SUPPRESSION_REVIEW_SIGNAL
- SUPPRESSION_STRONG_SIGNAL
- REVIEW_ESCALATION_REQUIRED
- GOVERNANCE_UNPROVEN

posture_meaning:
- PROMOTION_READY is reserved for later evidence-rich cases and is not broadly claimed in E9.
- PROMOTION_CANDIDATE means the family-context is structurally coherent enough for later promotion review, but not for runtime activation.
- MAINTAIN_AS_IS means keep the current governance standing without stronger promotion or suppression pressure.
- TIGHTENING_RECOMMENDED means later governance should require stronger conditions before broader family acceptance.
- NARROWING_RECOMMENDED means later governance should reduce the acceptable scope of the family-context.
- DOWNGRADE_RECOMMENDED means later governance should reduce standing or confidence if weakness persists.
- SUPPRESSION_REVIEW_SIGNAL means later suppression review is warranted, but not yet strong enough to be treated as the dominant governance posture.
- SUPPRESSION_STRONG_SIGNAL means later governance should treat suppression pressure as materially visible and in need of review.
- REVIEW_ESCALATION_REQUIRED means later review-board style attention is required before the family can remain unchanged.
- GOVERNANCE_UNPROVEN means the family-context is too lightly supported for stronger governance postures.

interpretation_rules:
- governance posture is derived from multiple evidence layers, never from one field alone.
- promotion posture must not bypass fragility, poor geometry, weak evidence depth, or overblocking risk.
- suppression posture must not bypass strategic-value review where tightening or narrowing may be more appropriate.
- governance posture in E9 does not mutate runtime state.
