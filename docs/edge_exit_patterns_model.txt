exit_families_defined:
- ROTATION_REVERSION_EXIT
- STRUCTURAL_CONTINUATION_EXIT
- BREAKOUT_STAGING_EXIT
- POST_DISPLACEMENT_HOLD_EXIT
- MICRO_EXPANSION_FAST_EXIT
- EXHAUSTION_PROTECTIVE_EXIT

family_definitions:

ROTATION_REVERSION_EXIT:
- suited_to: mean-reclaim and liquidity-reversal style families
- target_logic: edge-to-mid or return-to-range first; opposing-side stretch only if rotation quality remains clean
- partial_logic: early partial reduction is often justified once structural payback begins
- time_stop_logic: yes, especially when reclaim holds but rotation stalls
- failure_to_expand_logic: visible when reversal/reclaim stops rotating despite holding locally
- protective_tightening: increases as move approaches range interior or opposing liquidity

STRUCTURAL_CONTINUATION_EXIT:
- suited_to: continuation and pullback-continuation families
- target_logic: staged continuation objectives with structure-aware hold bias
- partial_logic: secondary, not purely mechanical
- time_stop_logic: used when continuation fails to advance in reasonable time
- failure_to_expand_logic: present when continuation stops separating after confirmation
- protective_tightening: follows structural trail and momentum decay

BREAKOUT_STAGING_EXIT:
- suited_to: breakout and release families
- target_logic: first impulse objective first, second-stage target only if acceptance persists
- partial_logic: prominent after initial release
- time_stop_logic: important when breakout remains alive but cannot travel
- failure_to_expand_logic: central
- protective_tightening: should become aggressive after first clean impulse

POST_DISPLACEMENT_HOLD_EXIT:
- suited_to: post-displacement continuation families
- target_logic: hold while post-displacement efficiency remains intact
- partial_logic: optional after strong displacement travel
- time_stop_logic: appears when reentry is present but continuation never restarts cleanly
- failure_to_expand_logic: important
- protective_tightening: responds to momentum decay and fatigue

MICRO_EXPANSION_FAST_EXIT:
- suited_to: micro-range expansion families
- target_logic: local/fast objectives, not broad travel assumptions
- partial_logic: may effectively coincide with first objective
- time_stop_logic: strict
- failure_to_expand_logic: dominant
- protective_tightening: early and aggressive

EXHAUSTION_PROTECTIVE_EXIT:
- suited_to: exhaustion-sensitive reversal environments
- target_logic: protect after fast relief move or exhaustion snapback
- partial_logic: favored when reversal already paid back local imbalance
- time_stop_logic: short leash
- failure_to_expand_logic: visible when snapback immediately stalls
- protective_tightening: prominent

architecture_rules:
- one exit family may still produce different state eligibility under different regimes
- exit families do not imply execution permission
- exit families do not imply score superiority
- exit family assignment is conceptual and may later be refined by scorecard or test evidence
