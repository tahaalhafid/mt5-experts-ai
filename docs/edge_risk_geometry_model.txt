EDGE RISK GEOMETRY MODEL
Authority: Institutional Edge Factory risk-geometry model only.
Truth posture: Geometry interpretation only. No runtime behavior change.

============================================================
WHAT RISK GEOMETRY MEANS
============================================================
Risk geometry means:
- where the thesis would logically fail
- how far invalidation sits from the intended entry logic
- how much clean path remains toward likely objective or rotation target
- whether nearby opposing liquidity, structure, or crowding degrades asymmetry
- whether the opportunity is being considered too late

Geometry therefore depends on:
- distance-to-invalidation
- distance-to-target
- crowding or toxic context
- structural clarity of the thesis
- freshness of the move

Thesis quality and geometry quality are related but not identical.
A thesis may be good while geometry is poor.
A trigger may be valid while asymmetry is unattractive.

============================================================
STOP-LOCATION LOGIC (CONCEPTUAL ONLY)
============================================================
A conceptual stop location should sit:
- beyond the thesis-failure point
- beyond reclaim-loss where reclaim matters
- beyond hold failure where hold matters
- beyond failed acceptance where acceptance matters
- beyond failed release where compression/release matters

A conceptual stop location should NOT sit:
- inside ordinary noise if the thesis expects noise
- inside the body of the defended structure without thesis reason
- at arbitrary tight distances chosen only for attractive math
- in a location that ignores nearby structure and crowding

============================================================
GEOMETRY POSTURE CLASSES
============================================================
- GEOMETRY_FAVORABLE
  The thesis is structurally clear and the path to target appears meaningfully better than the path to invalidation.

- GEOMETRY_ACCEPTABLE
  The opportunity is not ideal, but the distance-to-invalidation and path-to-objective remain structurally reasonable.

- GEOMETRY_TIGHT_BUT_FRAGILE
  Geometry can work only if the local hold/reclaim/release logic is very precise. False precision risk is elevated.

- GEOMETRY_CROWDED
  The thesis may remain valid, but nearby opposing liquidity, late expansion, or obvious crowding reduces edge quality.

- GEOMETRY_POOR
  The thesis may be interesting, but reward-to-risk or invalidation distance is structurally unattractive.

- GEOMETRY_NOT_APPLICABLE
  Under suppressive posture or non-licensable structure, geometry should not be treated as actionable.

============================================================
ADVERSE-SELECTION VISIBILITY
============================================================
Adverse-selection risk rises when:
- entry is attempted after the clean move is already spent
- nearby opposing liquidity leaves little room
- structure is degraded or dirty
- breakout is obvious and crowded
- reversal is attempted against still-active initiative
- micro expansion depends on fragile local precision

============================================================
DISCIPLINE
============================================================
- One good trigger does not rescue bad geometry.
- Poor reward-to-risk posture must remain visible.
- Tight geometry is not the same as good geometry.
- If target clarity is weak, geometry confidence should remain conservative.
- Geometry remains factory-level interpretation only until later scorecard and governance phases.
