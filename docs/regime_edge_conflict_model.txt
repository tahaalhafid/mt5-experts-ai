REGIME-EDGE CONFLICT MODEL
Authority: Institutional Edge Factory regime conflict visibility only.
Truth posture: Conflict visibility and caution only. No runtime behavior change.

============================================================
CONFLICT TYPES
============================================================
- ZONE_REGIME_MISMATCH
  A family belongs to a zone whose regime fit under current conditions is weak or suppressed.

- CONDITIONAL_CLUSTERING
  Too many families remain conditional inside one regime, creating overpermission pressure.

- SUPPORT_HEAVY_DEPENDENCY
  A regime depends mostly on support logic or contextual overlays rather than clear family seeds.

- OVERLAP_PRESSURE
  Too many adjacent zones compete inside the same regime without clear separation.

- SPARSE_COVERAGE
  A regime has weak or missing primary/secondary family representation.

- OVERPERMISSION_RISK
  A regime accumulates too many non-suppressed families and risks becoming a permissive catch-all bucket.

============================================================
HOW TO READ CONFLICT STATUS
============================================================
Conflict visibility does NOT mean:
- the regime is unusable
- the family is invalid
- runtime suppression has already happened

Conflict visibility means:
- later licensing/testing phases must tighten permission discipline
- narrative plausibility alone is not enough
- structural compatibility must remain narrower than label overlap

============================================================
ANTI-OVERCLAIM RULE
============================================================
A regime with many attached families is not automatically a strong regime.
Sometimes it is simply a sign that the factory still needs sharper separation and stricter permissioning.
