GLOBAL REVIEW PRINCIPLES
- AI outputs remain derived, bounded, reviewable artifacts.
- AI authority remains limited to AI_OFF, AI_SHADOW_ONLY, and AI_ADVISORY_ONLY.
- No human review state or decision in this document creates direct runtime mutation authority.
- No proposal approval in this workflow equals live activation.
- No learning evidence acceptance in this workflow mutates runtime or plan truth.
- Council advisory packets remain contextual, bounded, non-directional, and non-authoritative.
- Repository storage and human review are connected conceptually, but A7 does not implement full runtime workflow tooling.

ROLE MODEL
- OPERATOR = reads operational outputs, acknowledges informational items, and flags review-worthy items.
- ADMIN_REVIEWER = performs first-line formal review for incidents, daily intelligence, bounded proposals, and learning evidence.
- REVIEW_BOARD = reviews bounded proposals, learning-governance evidence, repeated drift patterns, and structured improvement candidates.
- COMMITTEE_REVIEWER = reviews high-level executive/governance items and major escalations.

STATE RULES
- INFORMATIONAL_ONLY items remain archival/visible by default and do not silently become approval objects.
- PENDING_REVIEW must identify the review trigger or reason for review-worthiness.
- UNDER_REVIEW must preserve reviewer role and decision context.
- ACCEPTED_FOR_ACTION means governed next step only; it never means direct runtime mutation, direct plan mutation, or live activation.
- ACCEPTED_FOR_EXPERIMENT means review-approved bounded experiment or implementation-planning handoff only; it never means auto-apply.
- DEFERRED must retain a reason such as awaiting more evidence, lower urgency, or superseding review window.
- REJECTED should preserve the rejection reason for institutional memory.
- ESCALATED must record target role and escalation reason.
- CLOSED_NO_ACTION closes the current review cycle and prevents endless open-loop review drift.

OUTPUT-TO-REVIEW NORMALIZATION
- Informational by default: HOURLY_WATCH_REPORT, DAILY_OPERATING_INTELLIGENCE_REPORT, OPERATOR_COPILOT_RESPONSE_TEMPLATE, EXECUTIVE_ADMIN_BRIEF, COUNCIL_ADVISORY_PACKET_TEMPLATE.
- Reviewable by operator/admin depending on severity or relevance: EVENT_TRIGGERED_INCIDENT_REPORT, GOVERNANCE_AUDIT_OUTPUT, ROOT_CAUSE_OUTPUT, ANOMALY_OUTPUT, DRIFT_OUTPUT.
- Review-board required: REVIEWABLE_PROPOSAL_TEMPLATE, LEARNING_GOVERNANCE_EVIDENCE_TEMPLATE.
- Committee-visible only by default: EXECUTIVE_ADMIN_BRIEF and major governance escalations.
- Escalation-eligible when repeated/high-severity/governance-relevant patterns exist: incidents, drift deterioration, governance contradictions, repeated advisory hold patterns with measurable effect.

- ADMIN_REVIEWER may reject, defer, request more evidence, or escalate to REVIEW_BOARD.

- Any accepted outcome remains human-governed and separate from runtime application.

policy_notes=
- Council advisory packets are operator-visible and archived.
- Council advisory packets do not require formal live-moment approval bureaucracy.
- Council advisory packets may later be reviewed for effectiveness, consistency, or hold-pattern consequences.
- Council advisory packets may be referenced as supporting evidence in incident, root-cause, proposal, drift, or learning workflows.
- Council advisory packets do not by themselves authorize action, runtime control, or directional trading behavior.

ESCALATION RULES
- Repeated HIGH or CRITICAL incident patterns should escalate to ADMIN_REVIEWER.
- Repeated drift deterioration, recurring anomaly clusters, or repeated review-relevant learning evidence should escalate to REVIEW_BOARD.
- Bounded proposals affecting learning/governance review should escalate to REVIEW_BOARD.
- Major governance contradiction, repeated cross-role disagreement, or high-level institutional risk note should escalate to COMMITTEE_REVIEWER.
- Recurring council advisory hold/reevaluation patterns with measurable operational effect should escalate into post-fact effectiveness review.
- Escalation never grants direct runtime authority; it only changes the human review venue.

CLOSURE RULES
- Informational note acknowledged and archived -> CLOSED_NO_ACTION.
- Incident investigated with no governed next step -> CLOSED_NO_ACTION.
- Proposal rejected -> CLOSED_NO_ACTION with rejection reason preserved.
- Proposal accepted for experiment or governed action handoff -> closed within the review workflow after handoff record is created.
- Evidence bundle superseded by newer evidence -> CLOSED_NO_ACTION with supersession note.
- Drift/anomaly signal expired without further significance -> CLOSED_NO_ACTION.
- Escalated item closes only after the target review role records a terminal outcome.

REPOSITORY RELATIONSHIP
- The repository stores AI outputs and review-relevant artifacts.
- The human review workflow determines how review-worthy outputs move through states and decisions.
- Review history belongs conceptually to repository-linked governance history, but repository storage remains non-authoritative and derived.
- A7 does not redesign repository persistence architecture or build runtime workflow tooling.
