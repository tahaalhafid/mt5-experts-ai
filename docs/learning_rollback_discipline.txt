1. FORMAL ROLE OF ROLLBACK DISCIPLINE
Rollback discipline in this phase ensures that no learning output is treated as promotion-ready unless a credible rollback posture exists.
This phase does not execute rollback.
This phase does not authorize rollback by itself.
This phase only defines expectations that must already be satisfied before promotion readiness may be recognized.

2. ROLLBACK TRIGGER TYPES
rollback_trigger_type=UNEXPECTED_DOWNSIDE_EXCEEDS_BOUNDED_EXPECTATION
rollback_trigger_examples=NEGATIVE_BEHAVIOR_OUTSIDE_DECLARED_TOLERANCE|WORSE_THAN_EXPECTED_REVIEWED_RESULT|MATERIAL_UNDERPERFORMANCE

rollback_trigger_type=EVIDENCE_INVALIDATION
rollback_trigger_examples=SOURCE_LINKAGE_FAILURE|POST_REVIEW_CONTRADICTION|LATER_EVIDENCE_REVERSAL|DISCOVERED_DATA_DEFECT

rollback_trigger_type=REVIEW_GOVERNANCE_CONTRADICTION
rollback_trigger_examples=SIGNOFF_MISMATCH|SCOPE_MISMATCH|DECISION_HISTORY_CONFLICT|MISSING_APPROVAL_PATH

rollback_trigger_type=PROPOSAL_OR_EXPERIMENT_OUTCOME_MATERIAL_UNDERPERFORMANCE
rollback_trigger_examples=SUCCESS_CRITERIA_NOT_MET|FAILURE_CONDITION_REACHED|UNCERTAINTY_GREATER_THAN_ALLOWED

rollback_trigger_type=HARMFUL_SIDE_EFFECT_EMERGENCE
rollback_trigger_examples=UNEXPECTED_SYSTEM_BEHAVIOR|UNACCEPTABLE_SECONDARY_RISK|GOVERNANCE_OR_SAFETY_COST_TOO_HIGH

rollback_trigger_type=GOVERNANCE_BOUNDARY_BREACH
rollback_trigger_examples=AUTHORITY_SCOPE_DRIFT|IMPLICIT_MUTATION_PATH|REVIEW_BYPASS|FORBIDDEN_OUTPUT_SURFACE_USE

rollback_trigger_type=OBSERVABILITY_OR_DIAGNOSTIC_CONTRADICTION
rollback_trigger_examples=MONITORING_MISMATCH|ROOT_CAUSE_REVERSAL|VALIDATION_CONTRADICTION_DISCOVERED_LATER

3. ROLLBACK READINESS REQUIREMENTS
A promotion candidate must declare all of the following:
- rollback_owner_role
- rollback_scope
- rollback_trigger_conditions
- rollback_feasibility_note
- rollback_traceability_reference
- rollback_boundary_note

rollback_owner_role_expectation=HUMAN_GOVERNANCE_ROLE_DEFINED_BEFORE_PROMOTION_READINESS
rollback_scope_expectation=EXACT_BOUNDARY_OF_POTENTIAL_REVERSION_OR_WITHDRAWAL_MUST_BE_DECLARED
rollback_trigger_expectation=TRIGGER_CONDITIONS_MUST_BE_SPECIFIC_AND_REVIEWABLE
rollback_feasibility_expectation=FEASIBILITY_NOTE_MUST_ACKNOWLEDGE_LIMITATIONS_AND_DEPENDENCIES
rollback_traceability_expectation=ROLLBACK_REFERENCES_MUST_LINK_TO_RELEVANT_EVIDENCE_REVIEW_AND_PROMOTION_RECORDS
rollback_boundary_expectation=ROLLBACK_MUST_NOT_CREATE_NEW_MUTATION_PATH_OR_AUTHORITY_EXPANSION

4. ROLLBACK DECISION EXPECTATIONS
Rollback consideration should be prompt when trigger conditions are met or credibly threatened.
Rollback consideration should be traceable, bounded, and sign-off-aware.
Rollback consideration should prefer governance safety over narrative optimism.
Rollback consideration in this phase remains conceptual and preparatory only.

5. ROLLBACK SIGN-OFF EXPECTATIONS
REVIEW_BOARD is the default sign-off level for rollback readiness sufficiency at promotion stage.
COMMITTEE_REVIEWER visibility or sign-off may be required when governance sensitivity or materiality justifies it.
No rollback readiness declaration permits autonomous rollback execution.

6. NON-NEGOTIABLE RULE
A promotion candidate without credible rollback readiness is not promotion-ready.
A vague promise to roll back later does not satisfy this phase.
A missing rollback owner, trigger set, or traceability reference is a promotion blocker.
