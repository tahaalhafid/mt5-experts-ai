purpose:
- define when later review escalation is conceptually required for edge families
- preserve governance-only behavior in E9

escalation_triggers:
- strong suppression signal with meaningful strategic value
- promotion ambiguity in a family that still appears strategically important
- repeated tightening and narrowing pressure without clean resolution
- downgrade pressure combined with still-material factory relevance
- poor geometry interacting with attractive trigger logic in a current core family
- vague timing, invalidation, or exit logic that persists in a family otherwise carrying strategic weight

escalation_guidance:
- escalate rather than silently promote when structural coherence conflicts with weak evidence depth
- escalate rather than silently suppress when the family may still justify narrower or tighter use
- escalate when current core status and current evidence posture visibly diverge
- escalate conditional families when repeated fragility is present but strategic theme value remains non-trivial

hard_boundaries:
- escalation in E9 is only a review signal
- escalation does not disable runtime behavior
- escalation does not promote or suppress live
- escalation does not bypass later review governance
