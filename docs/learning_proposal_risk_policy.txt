RISK CLASSES
- LOW_RISK_REVIEW
  belongs_here=Observation-only and instrumentation proposals with narrow scope and no implied live logic effect.
  rationale=These proposals usually improve understanding or observability rather than propose sensitive change subjects.
  minimum_review_expectation=ADMIN_REVIEWER
  escalation_expectation=Escalate only if cross-scope or governance ambiguity emerges.
  experiment_only_handling_preferred=false

- MEDIUM_RISK_REVIEW
  belongs_here=Investigation proposals and other bounded review requests that may shape later decisions but do not yet seek sensitive review subjects.
  rationale=These proposals can affect workload, prioritization, or future interpretation and therefore need clearer framing.
  minimum_review_expectation=ADMIN_REVIEWER
  escalation_expectation=Escalate to REVIEW_BOARD when repeated patterns, cross-family implications, or policy sensitivity appear.
  experiment_only_handling_preferred=false

- HIGH_RISK_REVIEW
  belongs_here=Experiment-candidate and threshold-review proposals that touch sensitive interpretation, validation burden, or later controlled change preparation.
  rationale=These proposals can influence later governed change pathways and therefore require stronger evidence discipline.
  minimum_review_expectation=REVIEW_BOARD
  escalation_expectation=May require committee visibility if cross-governance conflict appears.
  experiment_only_handling_preferred=true

- GOVERNANCE_SENSITIVE_REVIEW
  belongs_here=Policy-review, gating-review, learning-scope tightening, learning-scope narrowing, or any proposal that could affect authority interpretation, scope boundaries, or review governance.
  rationale=Governance-sensitive proposals can reshape how later learning is handled even without touching runtime logic directly.
  minimum_review_expectation=REVIEW_BOARD
  escalation_expectation=Escalate to COMMITTEE_REVIEWER when institutional boundary questions or authority concerns arise.
  experiment_only_handling_preferred=case_dependent

RISK DISCIPLINE RULES
- Higher apparent sophistication does not lower risk class.
- Broad scope raises risk class.
- Weak evidence supporting a strong claim raises review caution and may disqualify the proposal.
- Governance sensitivity may outweigh narrow technical scope.
- Risk class assignment must be consistent with the proposal family and actual claim scope.

PROPOSAL ELIGIBILITY VS RISK
- Low risk does not excuse missing boundedness.
- High-quality evidence does not remove review requirements.
- Strong evidence may support stronger review attention, but not direct application.
- Governance-sensitive proposals require explicit mutation prohibition and scope boundaries.

PRE-REVIEW REJECTION CONDITIONS
Reject or return a draft before formal review packaging when any of the following is true:
- insufficient evidence for the claimed scope
- unresolved ambiguity is too high
- direct or hidden mutation intent is present
- boundedness is missing or vague
- evidence bundle is untraceable
- risk framing omits obvious governance sensitivity
- validation intent is absent
- rollback note is absent where needed
- the proposal uses broad performance rhetoric without exact scope
- the proposal implies authority expansion, direct control, or live changes

REVIEW PREPARATION REQUIREMENTS
Before a proposal is considered review-ready, it should clearly state:
- why this proposal family is the narrowest valid match
- why the chosen risk class is appropriate
- what evidence bundle supports it
- what limitation or ambiguity remains
- why review is warranted now
- what next step is suggested
- why the proposal remains non-mutative even if accepted for later handling

RELATIONSHIP TO LATER PHASES
This risk policy prepares proposals for later review-governed learning handling.
It does not authorize:
- experiment launch
- runtime mutation
- live threshold changes
- policy changes by itself
- rollback or promotion by itself

FINAL RULE
Learning proposals remain bounded, reviewable, and non-authoritative.
