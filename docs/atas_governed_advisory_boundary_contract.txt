ATAS Governed Advisory Boundary Contract (Level 3)
Version: 1
Language: English only

1) Runtime authority boundary
- MT5 runtime remains the sole authority for decision, execution, risk, and governance.
- ATAS governed advisory is non-authoritative contextual intelligence only.
- No ATAS advisory output may directly approve, deny, send, cancel, size, or risk-govern trades.

2) External boundary
- ATAS remains external shadow-derived input.
- Semantic adapter remains mandatory.
- No raw external feed enters MT5 directly.

3) Support/resistance boundary
- Canonical support/resistance authority remains MT5-internal.
- ATAS level evidence is bounded confluence/context only.
- ATAS cannot replace canonical level ownership.

4) Influence boundary
- Allowed bounded influence channels:
  - caution bias
  - reevaluation bias
  - hold-for-reevaluation bias (bounded rollout mode only)
  - confidence shaping
  - contradiction signaling
  - confirmation support
- Forbidden channels:
  - direct BUY/SELL generation
  - direction replacement
  - direct block/allow control authority
  - runtime parameter mutation authority

5) Scope lock for this implementation
- Databento remains inactive.
- Fusion remains inactive.
- Hybrid live reactivation is out of scope.
- AI advisory layer remains separate and preserved.

6) Safety fallback
- If advisory is unavailable or ineligible, runtime falls back to MT5 canonical flow.
- No hidden penalty is applied solely because advisory is absent.
