============================================================
WHAT MARKET-NATIVE INTELLIGENCE MEANS
============================================================
Market-native intelligence means:
- raw intelligence derived from the market itself
- price behavior, structure, liquidity interaction, displacement behavior, volatility behavior, and session behavior
- a higher-priority raw source layer than external playbooks, community ideas, or indicator-first narratives
- a factory layer that describes what the market is doing before deciding what should later become an edge

Market-native intelligence is NOT:
- a rebranding of indicator identity
- an inherited strategy name
- a runtime family by itself
- a trigger license
- a canon admission result

Boundary rules:
1) market-native intelligence is a raw source layer, not runtime strategy authority
2) market-native intelligence does not imply canon admission
3) market-native intelligence does not imply trigger approval
4) market-native intelligence does not bypass decomposition, codifiability, testing, or later governance gates
5) market-native intelligence may carry provisional zone/regime affinity only

============================================================
SOURCE PRIORITY POSTURE
============================================================
Source-priority order inside the factory:
1) MARKET_NATIVE_SOURCE
2) INTERNAL_RUNTIME_SOURCE
3) INTERNAL_ANALYTICS_SOURCE
4) RESEARCH_SOURCE / PLAYBOOK_SOURCE / HUMAN_REVIEW_SOURCE
5) SCRIPT_SOURCE / COMMUNITY_IDEA_SOURCE

Interpretation:
- the market itself is the primary raw teacher
- internal runtime and internal analytics remain high-value supporting evidence
- external playbooks and community sources remain supportive, not sovereign
- quantity of external material must never outrank clear market-native structure


============================================================
RELATIONSHIP TO OTHER SOURCE TYPES
============================================================
Market-native intelligence differs from:
- external playbooks/research/scripts
  These are interpretation layers or imported idea layers. They may support later comparison, but they do not outrank the market itself.

- internal self-data / runtime findings
  These are high-value internal observations about what the current system has done or discovered. They remain important, but they are not the same thing as raw market-native object identity.

- indicator primitives
  Indicators may later support confirmation, timing, or filtering. They do not define market-native object identity.

Rule:
- the market teaches first
- internal self-data helps verify, compare, and sharpen
- external materials may support, challenge, or inspire
- none of these may bypass later factory gates

============================================================
ANTI-INDICATOR-IDENTITY RULE
============================================================
Rule:
- market-native intelligence must be rooted in market behavior itself, not in indicator naming.
- indicator primitives may support later codification or confirmation logic.
- indicator primitives must not be allowed to masquerade as market-native object identity.

Examples:
- "sweep and reclaim at range edge" may be market-native
- "RSI oversold reversal" is not market-native identity; at most it may later become a support primitive
- "volatility contraction followed by acceptance after break" may be market-native
- "Bollinger touch mean reversion" is not automatically market-native identity
