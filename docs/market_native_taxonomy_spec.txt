MARKET-NATIVE TAXONOMY SPEC
Authority: Compact taxonomy for Institutional Edge Factory market-native intelligence.
Truth posture: Taxonomy specification only. No runtime behavior change.

============================================================
DESIGN PRINCIPLES
============================================================
- taxonomy must be market-behavior first
- taxonomy must remain operationally useful
- taxonomy must be compact enough for later decomposition/testing/canon work
- taxonomy must not devolve into indicator aliases
- taxonomy classes must remain interpretable across current and future sources

============================================================
CATEGORY A — LIQUIDITY-EVENT CLASSES
============================================================
- LOCAL_SWEEP_HIGH
  Local liquidity raid above recent obvious highs.

- LOCAL_SWEEP_LOW
  Local liquidity raid below recent obvious lows.

- EXTERNAL_RANGE_RAID
  Raid through external range boundary liquidity.

- INTERNAL_STOP_SWEEP
  Internal stop-cleaning event inside a broader structure.

- FAILED_SWEEP
  Sweep that fails to produce meaningful acceptance or continuation.

- SWEEP_AND_RECLAIM
  Sweep followed by reclaim back through the violated level/edge.

- SWEEP_AND_CONTINUATION
  Sweep that resolves into initiative continuation.

- LIQUIDITY_VACUUM_MOVE
  Fast movement through thin liquidity with weak opposing participation.

- LIQUIDITY_ABSORPTION_SIGNAL
  Evidence that aggression is being absorbed at a key liquidity area.

============================================================
CATEGORY B — STRUCTURE-QUALITY CLASSES
============================================================
- CLEAN_TREND_STRUCTURE
  Ordered directional structure with relatively coherent pullback hierarchy.

- NOISY_TREND_STRUCTURE
  Directional structure exists but is cluttered, unstable, or degraded.

- RANGE_WITH_CLEAN_EDGES
  Range with readable boundaries and repeatable edge behavior.

- DEGRADED_RANGE_STRUCTURE
  Range-like environment with weak edge clarity or repeated overshoot degradation.

- TRANSITION_STRUCTURE
  Structure is changing regime and directional quality is unresolved.

- POST_DISPLACEMENT_STRUCTURE
  Structure immediately after a meaningful displacement event.

- COMPRESSED_STRUCTURE
  Price compression with stored expansion potential.

- FRAGMENTED_STRUCTURE
  Broken, chopped, or incoherent structure unsuitable for clean edge framing.

============================================================
CATEGORY C — DISPLACEMENT / RECLAIM CLASSES
============================================================
- INITIATIVE_DISPLACEMENT
  Strong initiative move with clear market displacement character.

- WEAK_DISPLACEMENT
  Break-like movement without strong initiative quality.

- RECLAIM_AFTER_SWEEP
  Reclaim sequence following a liquidity sweep event.

- RECLAIM_AFTER_BREAK
  Reclaim sequence after a structural break or acceptance failure.

- FAILED_RECLAIM
  Reclaim attempt that does not hold.

- IMPULSE_WITHOUT_ACCEPTANCE
  Impulse move that lacks meaningful acceptance afterward.

- ACCEPTANCE_AFTER_BREAK
  Post-break acceptance that supports continuation framing.

============================================================
CATEGORY D — SESSION / VOLATILITY CLASSES
============================================================
- ASIAN_RANGE_BEHAVIOR
  Session behavior centered on contained range formation or pre-expansion balance.

- LONDON_EXPANSION_BEHAVIOR
  Session behavior centered on London-session expansion potential.

- NEWYORK_CONTINUATION_BEHAVIOR
  Session behavior centered on New York continuation or directional extension.

- OVERLAP_EXPANSION
  Expansion behavior emerging during key session overlap.

- DEAD_HOURS_DRIFT
  Low-quality directional drift during structurally weak hours.

- VOLATILITY_CONTRACTION
  Compression or contraction state.

- VOLATILITY_EXPANSION
  Expanding volatility state with greater movement potential.

- UNSTABLE_NOISY_VOLATILITY
  Volatility is elevated but poorly structured and not cleanly usable.

- NEWS_LIKE_ABNORMAL_VOLATILITY
  Abnormal volatility resembling event-shock conditions.

============================================================
CATEGORY E — TOXIC-CONTEXT CLASSES
============================================================
- BREAKOUT_INTO_NEAR_OPPOSING_LIQUIDITY
  Breakout path is immediately constrained by nearby opposing liquidity.

- OVEREXTENDED_BREAKOUT
  Breakout is already overly extended relative to its clean opportunity window.

- REVERSAL_ATTEMPT_AGAINST_ACTIVE_INITIATIVE
  Countermove attempts fight still-active initiative.

- CONTINUATION_WITHOUT_STRUCTURE_RESET
  Continuation attempt occurs without enough reset/pullback/context repair.

- FADE_BEFORE_LIQUIDITY_TARGET_COMPLETION
  Fade attempt appears before the more obvious liquidity objective is completed.

- BREAKOUT_WITHOUT_EXPANSION_CONFIRMATION
  Breakout attempt lacks expansion/displacement/acceptance support.

============================================================
OBJECT ROLE ALIGNMENT
============================================================
Typical primary roles:
- liquidity-event classes -> RAW_MARKET_EVENT or LIQUIDITY_CONTEXT_OBJECT
- structure-quality classes -> STRUCTURAL_CONTEXT_OBJECT
- displacement/reclaim classes -> DISPLACEMENT_CONTEXT_OBJECT
- session/volatility classes -> SESSION_CONTEXT_OBJECT or VOLATILITY_CONTEXT_OBJECT
- toxic-context classes -> TOXICITY_SUPPRESSION_OBJECT

============================================================
PROVISIONAL AFFINITY RULE
============================================================
Taxonomy classes may carry:
- provisional zone fit
- provisional regime fit
- directional_bias_hint

Taxonomy classes may NOT carry:
- canon admission
- runtime licensing
- final trigger authority
