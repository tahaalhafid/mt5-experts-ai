EDGE ENRICHMENT LOOP POLICY
phase_id=E10
policy_role=CONTINUOUS_EDGE_ENRICHMENT_GOVERNANCE_ONLY

1) PURPOSE
Continuous enrichment means governed re-entry of new evidence, new market-native findings, new raw sources, scorecard/governance outputs, and bounded internal findings back into the factory.
It does NOT mean live self-modification.
It does NOT mean automatic runtime mutation.
It does NOT mean authority expansion.

2) WHAT ENRICHMENT IS
- controlled continuation of the factory lifecycle
- bounded re-entry into intake, decomposition, review, governance, rescoping, research, or archive paths
- evidence-based refinement without bypassing factory stages

3) WHAT ENRICHMENT IS NOT
- runtime mutation
- direct promotion
- direct suppression
- direct runtime export
- live parameter rewriting
- autonomous self-learning control

4) ALLOWED ENRICHMENT INPUTS
- new market-native findings
- new raw sources
- new decomposition outputs
- scorecard review signals
- runtime accounting findings
- deterioration signals
- suppression or narrowing signals
- governance review outcomes
- internal post-trade / post-decision findings
- AI decomposition or research outputs only within current bounded authority

5) ANTI-MUTATION RULE
All E10 enrichment remains non-runtime, non-mutative, non-executing, and non-licensing.
Any enrichment signal must re-enter through a governed factory stage, not bypass it.

6) ANTI-BLOAT RULE
More loop signals do not equal more edge value.
Repeated low-value re-entry should remain visible as loop noise rather than being reframed as progress.
